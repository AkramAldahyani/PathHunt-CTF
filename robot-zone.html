Robots Recon

Category: Recon · Technique: robots.txt inspection
Brief

Every well-behaved website can ship a robots.txt file at its root. Its job: tell search-engine crawlers which paths they should not index. It's a gentleman's agreement, not a security control.

That makes it a gift for hunters. Admins list the exact paths they don't want indexed — which is a complete roadmap of pages they consider sensitive.

Hint The file in question is plain text and lives at a well-known, fixed location every site agrees on. No page links to it — you're expected to know the name and type it in. Once you're there, every line matters. Even the ones that look like decoration.
Technique

Real recon is just a list of well-known paths typed into the address bar one after another. Sitemaps, crawler hints, security disclosures, build manifests — they all live at predictable locations. None of them are linked from the homepage. All of them are public if you ask.

What the site tries to hide from Google is often exactly what you want to see.
